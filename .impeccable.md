## Design Context

### Users
Samsung TV owners using a remote from living-room distance to discover live and upcoming sports, inspect the teams and event status, choose among available feeds, and start playback with as few directional-key presses as possible. The core jobs are event discovery, quick filtering (all/live/today/upcoming and sport), reliable stream selection, and clear recovery when a feed fails.

### Brand Personality
Energetic, direct, and stadium-like. Preserve the SportzX identity and its playful confidence, but keep operational copy calm and concise when loading or playback fails. Live status should feel immediate without making the whole interface visually loud.

### Aesthetic Direction
A dark broadcast-guide interface adapted specifically for a 10-foot, 16:9 TV canvas: deep blue-black surfaces, warm off-white text, live red for time-sensitive state, and the existing indigo/magenta identity as a restrained focus accent. Use the bundled Nunito family for continuity. Prefer editorial rows and compact match boards over generic card grids. Focus must be unmistakable at distance, and all primary actions must work without pointer or touch input.

### Design Principles
- Design every state for five-way remote navigation, with deterministic focus restoration and no focus traps.
- Put live status, teams, start time, competition, and feed availability ahead of decoration.
- Preserve the source app's event semantics and recognizable visual identity while removing mobile-only and advertising flows.
- Keep playback immersive: controls appear on intent, explain the current feed, and recede quickly without obscuring the match.
- Treat network, decoding, empty, and unsupported-stream states as first-class TV screens with a clear retry or back path.
- Maintain overscan-safe spacing, large readable type, high contrast, reduced-motion support, and 1080p/4K scaling.
